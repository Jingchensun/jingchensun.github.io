Chapter 1: Data and Probability Review

Section 1.1: Introduction to Data

Statistics is driven by real applications:

  • Collect data
  • Understand what it means

Example (Flight Delays):

  • Columns = variables
  • Rows = observations

Section 1.2: Types of Data

  • Population: All individuals or observations
  • Sample: A subset of the population
  • Random sample: Selected randomly and independently

Statistical inference: Draw conclusions about the population from a sample.

Notation:

  • Random variables: \( X, Y, Z \)
  • Observed values: \( x_1, x_2, \ldots \)

Section 1.3: Observations

Random Process Observations

Infinite population. Observations are i.i.d.

Example (Bernoulli):

\( X \sim \text{Bern}(p) \):

\[ X = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{with probability } p \\ 0 & \text{with probability } 1 - p \end{cases} \]

Finite Population Observations

Population size \( N \) is finite and known.

Example: Population = \{1, 2, 3, 3̅, 7\}, \( N = 5 \)

  • With replacement: \{2, 3, 2\}
  • Without replacement: \{7, 3, 3̅\}

When \( N \gg n \), both sampling methods give similar results.

Section 1.4: Statistics vs Parameters

  • Statistic: Numerical characteristic of a sample
  • Parameter: Numerical characteristic of a population/distribution

Examples:

  • \( X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2) \): \( \mu, \sigma \) are parameters
  • Sample mean \( \bar{X} = \frac{1}{n} \sum X_i \): a statistic
  • Data mean \( \bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum x_i \): a statistic

Section 1.5: Surveys

Survey: Asking individuals their opinions/behaviors.

Sample Survey: Select a sample due to cost/time constraints.

Sampling process: Population → Sampling Frame → Random Sample

Example: General Social Survey (GSS)

  • Since 1970s; covers income, education, gender, etc.
  • Multilevel stratified sampling

Section 1.6: Study Types

Observational Study

No intervention; just observation.

Example: Beer and hot wings study

Experimental Study

Subjects receive treatments, randomly assigned to groups.

  • Treatment vs control groups

Example: Tree seedling and fertilizer experiment

Caution: Non-random samples limit generalizability.

Example: Tai Chi study based on volunteers only

Section 1.7: Fundamentals of Probability

Basic Definitions

  • Sample space \( S \): All possible outcomes
  • Discrete RV: Maps outcomes to a countable set
  • Continuous RV: Takes values in \( \mathbb{R} \)

Probability: Long-run frequency of events.

Conditional Probability

\( P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \)

Law of Total Probability

\( P(A) = \sum P(B_i) P(A|B_i) \) for partition \( \{B_i\} \)

PMF of Discrete RV

\( p(x) = P(X = x), \quad \sum p(x) = 1 \)

Binomial Distribution

\( p(k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1 - p)^{n-k} \)

Expectation and Variance

\( \mu = \mathbb{E}[X] \), \( \sigma^2 = \mathbb{E}[(X - \mu)^2] \)

PDF of Continuous RV

\( f(x) \geq 0 \), \( \int f(x) dx = 1 \)

CDF

\( F(x) = P(X \leq x) = \int_{-\infty}^{x} f(t) dt \)

Exponential Distribution

\( f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x} \), \( \mu = 1/\lambda \), \( \sigma^2 = 1/\lambda^2 \)

Variance Shortcuts

  • \( \text{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - \mu^2 \)
  • \( \text{Var}(a + bX) = b^2 \cdot \text{Var}(X) \)

Section 1.8: Sample Mean and Normal Distribution

Sample Mean

\( \bar{X} = \frac{1}{n} \sum X_i \) with \( \mathbb{E}[\bar{X}] = \mu \), \( \text{Var}[\bar{X}] = \frac{\sigma^2}{n} \)

Bernoulli Example

  • \( \mathbb{E}[\bar{X}] = p \)
  • \( \text{Var}[\bar{X}] = \frac{p(1 - p)}{n} \)

Normal Distribution

  • \( X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2) \)
  • Standard form: \( Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma} \)
  • \( \Phi(z) \) = CDF of standard normal
  • \( P(-1 < Z < 1) \approx 68\% \), \( \pm 2 \approx 95\% \), \( \pm 3 \approx 99.7\% \)

Normal Difference Example

\( X \sim \mathcal{N}(100, 25) \), \( Y \sim \mathcal{N}(90, 36) \)

\( P(X - Y > 6) \approx 0.695 \)

Sum of Normals and MGFs

Let \( X = \sum a_i X_i \), then:

  • \( \mu = \sum a_i \mu_i \)
  • \( \sigma^2 = \sum a_i^2 \sigma_i^2 \)

Moment Generating Function: \( M(t) = \mathbb{E}[e^{tX}] \)

\( \frac{d^n}{dt^n} M(t) \bigg|_{t = 0} = \mathbb{E}[X^n] \)
