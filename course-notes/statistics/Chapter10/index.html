Section 10: Categorical Data

10.1: Independence in Contingency Tables

Example: A survey of 200 students (HS and College) asks for preference between Instagram and Snapchat.

GroupInstaSnapRow Sum% Insta
HS346610034%
College524810052%
Total8611420043%

Hypotheses:

  • \( H_0 \): Instagram preference is independent of group
  • \( H_A \): Instagram preference depends on group

Expected Counts under \( H_0 \):

GroupInstaSnapRow Sum
HS4357100
College4357100
Total86114200

Chi-Squared Statistic:

\[ C = \sum_{\text{all cells}} \frac{(O - E)^2}{E} = 6.6 \]

Large \( C \Rightarrow \) large deviation between observed and expected → group matters.

p-value from chi-squared distribution:

\[ P(C \ge 6.6) = 0.01 \quad \text{(df = 1)} \]

Conclusion: Reject \( H_0 \). Group has an effect.

10.2: Permutation Test of Independence

Idea: If group and preference are independent, then any permutation of the preferences is equally likely.

Example: Education group vs Death Penalty preference (Favor or Oppose)

Steps:

  1. Randomly permute preference labels while keeping group labels fixed
  2. Recalculate chi-squared statistic \( C_i \) for each permutation
  3. Repeat to get empirical distribution: \( C_1, C_2, \dots, C_N \)

p-value:

\[ p = P(C \ge C_{\text{obs}}) \]

If observed \( C \) lies in the tail → reject independence

10.3: Chi-Square Test of Independence

Definition: A chi-square test assesses whether observed frequencies in a contingency table differ significantly from expected frequencies under the assumption of independence.

Test statistic:

\[ C = \sum \frac{(O - E)^2}{E} \]

Where:

  • \( O \): observed count in each cell
  • \( E \): expected count under independence

Degrees of Freedom:

\[ df = (r - 1)(c - 1) \quad \text{(where r = #rows, c = #columns)} \]

Distribution under \( H_0 \):

\[ C \sim \chi^2(df) \]

Use p-value from chi-square distribution to determine significance.

Decision:

  • If \( p \le \alpha \): Reject \( H_0 \), conclude dependence
  • If \( p > \alpha \): Fail to reject \( H_0 \)
